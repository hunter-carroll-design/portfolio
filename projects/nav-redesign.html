Optimizing Mobile Navigation:

Boosting Usability for a Seamless User Experience

Role:
  • UX/UI Design
  • UX Research
  • Front-end Development
Time:
  • 2 weeks
Tools/Skills:
  • Figma
  • HTML
  • CSS
  • VS Code

Problem

Upon reviewing the university website’s original design, I quickly identified that users faced significant navigation challenges. The hierarchical organization of information was confusing, and it was difficult for users to determine the relative importance of pages related to various offices, departments, and/or current topics. This resulted in a disjointed user experience, requiring extra effort to find essential information.

After determining these issues, three goals were set:

  • Make UI more intuitive
  • Establish a clear hierarchy
  • Design a visually appealing, brand-aligned navigation

The Original Design

The original navigation was cluttered and lacked a clear hierarchy, making it difficult for users to navigate. It was also visually unappealing, detracting from the overall user experience.

Primary Limitation:

Since this section of the website is static I was asked to only redesign the navigation using CSS without altering its position or HTML.

The Process

Step 1: Organized the types of links

The first step was to categorize the links to explore hierarchical options based on their folder locations. I divided the links into two categories: those selected by the department and all pages within the designated folder.

Step 2: Research and Inspiration

To gather ideas, I researched navigation designs on other university websites. This provided valuable insights into effective hierarchical structures and aesthetic approaches.

Step 3: Determined my limitations (CSS I could change)

Given my primary limitation of only using CSS, I utilized Chrome DevTools to identify exactly what I could modify. This step was crucial in determining the feasible design changes within the constraints.

Step 4: Designing Variations

With the constraints in mind, I created several design variations. I focused on adjusting the color scheme and balance to enhance visual appeal while maintaining functionality. The challenge was to make significant improvements within the static structure, emphasizing clarity and ease of use.

The Outcome

The redesigned navigation, while limited by the inability to change the HTML code, introduced several improvements:

  • Enhanced Hierarchy: A clear organization of links, making it easier for users to navigate through different sections.
  • Improved Visual Appeal: A cleaner, more cohesive look that aligns with the university’s branding.
  • Functional Adjustments: Although limited in scope, I proposed relocating the hamburger menu for better accessibility and adding an option to minimize internal pages of the office/folder.

Conclusion

This project demonstrated how even within strict limitations, thoughtful design and strategic use of CSS can significantly enhance user experience. By focusing on clear organization, intuitive navigation, and visual appeal, I was able to transform the university website’s navigation into a more user-friendly and aesthetically pleasing component.
