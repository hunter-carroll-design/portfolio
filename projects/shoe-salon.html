Shoe Salon:

Improving Customer Retention by Creating An Ecommerce Mobile Option

Problem

After reviewing the gathered information it was determined that the primary reason people feel uncomfortable using the Craigslist site is due to safety concerns and a hard time navigating/using the website.

Research/Empathize

Understanding the audience

Busy customers have expressed impatience with getting an online experience for Shoe Salon. Some have started moving to other footwear stores with an online shopping option. The majority of these customers are people with busy lives who would like to order online while also easily viewing the reviews of other shoppers.

Customers of Shoe Salon:
  • 1 - 80 years old
  • From infants to people with longtime retirees
  • From children to grandparents and single individuals
Active users of Shoe Salon:
  • 5 - 70 years old (excluding infants and seniors as most people will physically shop for them)
  • From young teens to people with longstanding careers
Target users of Shoe Salon Ecommerce Experience:
  • 14 - 50 years old
  • From teens to working individuals with busy schedules

User Journey Map

The Goal

Due to high demand from busy customers, Shoe Salon would like a mobile app that allows for easy purchasing, quality reviews and a fairly fast checkout time.

A mobile app was decided since customers who have little time will most likely look for products during breaks or meals. A mobile app will also bring in customers from young adults and teens.

The competition

In order to understand the best practices for the mobile app, I conducted a competitor audit on 3 apps for different companies. My results determined that the majority of competition utilize a minimalistic style, both for clarity on the flow and the easy visuals. The reviews are typically only accessible from the product page.

The competition

2 direct and 1 indirect competitor

The competitor audit provided to me that creating an easy and uncluttered experience is key as it well reduce information overload and decision paralysis.

Design/Ideate

When beginning the ideation stage I began by referencing the project goals:

  1. Create an uncluttered experience
  2. Provide easy way to find reviews
  3. Have a fast and easy checkout
  4. Lessen shopping time taken and assure quality

Later, when creating the digital wireframes, I expanded on the portions not directly related to the main flow, screens like the account page, menu, and search. There are also additions like "The Salon" which hosts a collection of images and reviews from different customers.

The digital wireframes below show these changes and the changes based on the first round of usability studies. This study identified user pain points and a couple missing screens.

Usability Study: Round 1

When beginning the ideation stage I began by referencing the project goals:

Prompt 1:

From the home screen DO NOT sign in but order a product.

  1. Create an uncluttered experience
Prompt 2:

Add a product to your cart, take it out then, find and add another one. Check out after.

  1. How easy or difficult was this task?
Prompt 3:

Decide on a product based on reviews and personal experiences.

  1. How difficult was completing this task?
  2. Did you have trouble finding reviews?

Q&A

  • What confuses you?

  • How difficult was it to order a product?

    • Why was it difficult or easy?

  • Did you get lost anywhere?

  • Did you use the “The Salon” portion of the app?

    • What did you use it for?

Usability Study: Round 2

Based on the high-fidelity prototype.

Utilizing the same questions and prompts as the first usability study the high-fidelity prototype was tested.

Changes from this study was based mostly on UI decisions that would create problems in the user experience.

MAJOR PROBLEM FOUND:

Accessibility - The color chose I made originally would have have limited accessibility, specifically towards colorblind individuals. This is due to the low contrast between the two colors. Also, when I tested the design myself the screen brightness affected the contrast with no difference between the colors.

Potential Outcomes

Customer Retention & Happiness

  • Customer retention may increase due to addressing customer concerns, becoming more modern, and providing transparency.

Lead Generation

  • The increase retention rate and happiness of customers will help spread the reach of the store. Whether in the form of reviews, or just by word-of-mouth, the outcome will be new and incoming customers.

Learnings

Re-Examine

  • Mid-way through this project I realized I was comparing my design a bit too much to the competitors. For example my wireframe I added a portion for deals, when I recognized this I decided to change that portion to order status. In order to fix this I re-examined my user personas and research to gain a focused mind on what the goal of the project was.

Remember the goal

  • Always ask what the user goals are; whether you are creating wireframes, checking over research, or designing visuals.
