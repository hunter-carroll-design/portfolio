Problem

The University’s visitor page lacked the engaging and welcoming design needed to leave a positive impression, leading to prospective students feeling less enthusiastic about visiting the University.

The Original Design

Goals:

  • Increase the number of visits to the University.
  • Create a Lasting First Impression
  • Boost CampusEngagement
  • Modernize the design while matching the marketing materials sent out to prospective students.

Reputation Research:

  • Safety Concerns: There have been instances of fraud, theft, and even violence related to transactions initiated through Craigslist.
  • User Experience: The website design of Craigslist is notoriously outdated and lacks modern features found on other online marketplaces. Specifically the lack of a robust search and filter system can make it challenging.
  • Unmoderated Content: Craigslist relies heavily on user-generated content, which means there is minimal moderation of listings. This can result in a wide range of listings, including spam, misleading advertisements, and inappropriate content.
  • Limited Customer Support: Craigslist offers unreliable customer support, which can be frustrating for users encountering issues with transactions, listings, or accounts.
  • Local Focus: It also means that users might have limited options compared to larger, nationwide marketplaces.

Product Page Research:

  • Outdated Design: The design of Craigslist's product pages is very basic and outdated, lacking modern aesthetics and features found on other online marketplaces. This can make the browsing experience feel clunky and less user-friendly.
  • Limited Information: Product listings on Craigslist typically contain limited information about the item being sold. Sellers are often encouraged to provide only essential details, which can make it challenging for buyers to make informed decisions.
  • Sparse Visuals: Craigslist's product pages usually feature minimal or no images of the items for sale.
  • Minimal Interactivity: Unlike other e-commerce platforms that offer features such as integrated messaging, payment processing, and user reviews, Craigslist's product pages lack interactivity. Users must rely on email or phone communication to negotiate transactions, which can be less convenient and secure.
  • Limited Search and Filter Options: Craigslist's search and filter options are relatively basic compared to other online marketplaces.

Competitor Research:

  • eBay: eBay Classifieds integrated with eBay's main platform offering local classified ads, allowing users to buy, sell, or trade a wide range of items.
  • OfferUp: OfferUp is a mobile marketplace that emphasizes local buying and selling. It features a user-friendly interface and offers features such as in-app messaging and user ratings to facilitate transactions.
  • Facebook Marketplace: Facebook Marketplace allows users to buy and sell items within their local community. It benefits from the large user base of Facebook and integrates with Messenger for communication between buyers and sellers.
  • Gumtree: Gumtree is a classified ads website available in various countries, including the UK, Australia, and South Africa. It offers a wide range of categories for buying and selling items locally.

The Content

The Communications Coordinator and Specialist selected content to emphasis faculty and students, student life, and tailored assistance.

This focus was divided into the following:

Immersive Campus Exploration:

The page invites prospective students to experience the university environment through guided tours, highlighting key locations such as academic buildings, the library, and student gathering spaces. This immersive approach helps students visualize their future at Texas A&M University–Kingsville (TAMUK) and builds excitement for their potential journey.

Connection with Faculty and Students:

By featuring opportunities to meet faculty and interact with current students, the page fosters a sense of belonging and community. This element strengthens trust and credibility, allowing prospective students to gain insight into the university’s academic and social environment firsthand.

Personalized and Supportive Experience:

The inclusion of student-led tours ensures that visitors receive guidance from peers who understand their concerns and interests. These ambassadors provide firsthand experiences, answer questions, and offer tailored recommendations, reinforcing the university’s commitment to student support.

Seamless Visit Planning:

A streamlined registration process encourages prospective students to schedule a campus visit with ease. Clear instructions and flexible options, including advance registration and walk-in policies, ensure accessibility while accommodating individual preferences.

Emotional and Aspirational Engagement:

The content is designed to help students see themselves thriving at TAMUK—attending classes, joining organizations, and forming lifelong connections. By fostering an emotional connection, the visit page plays a crucial role in influencing enrollment decisions.
